Reading ACE-BENCH: why cloud-edge agents need a three-axis benchmark.

The leaderboard tells you who wins by Completion Score. This research note explains what the score means, how the benchmark is built, and why privacy and cost change the deployment decision.

Research note Updated June 2026 Utility · Cost · Privacy

A single success rate is not enough for edge-cloud agents.

Agentic tasks are executed inside local workspaces, where files, logs, databases, browser state, and private context matter. Cloud models can improve completion, but every escalation has a resource and privacy footprint.

ACE-BENCH therefore reports each strategy as a profile rather than only a scalar rank: task completion, cloud-side usage, and risk-weighted privacy performance are shown together.

Key Findings

Cloud-only systems deliver strong task utility, but expose much more local context. Edge-only systems preserve privacy, yet lose capability on long-horizon workspace tasks.

Edge-cloud collaboration improves the operating point by selectively invoking cloud capability, but the result depends on when the cloud is used and what context is transmitted.

Stronger edge models shift the preferred pattern from step-level fallback toward task routing, planning, and recovery guidance.
